Backlinks

Backlinks are links on a website that point to other. Search engines, such as Google, They consider backlinks as votes of confidence. By That is, pages with many backlinks usually have a Higher positioning and authority on Google.

Our Backlinks tool provides a detailed analysis of the links that point to your site, allowing you to optimize your strategy desire. Below you will find a guide step by step on how to use this tool effectively
1
Login

Once logged in, go to the menu on the left side of the screen and Click on the 'Backlinks' option.
3
Enter URL for Analysis

4
Results

Types of REPORTS

Site Overview Report
The Backlinks tool will provide you with an analysis overview of your site's link profile, showing the following key metrics:
  • Weak Flow: Indicates the low quality of certain backlinks, which can signal opportunities for improvement.
  • Trust Flow: Measure the trust of links towards you site, based on the quality of the sites that link to you.
  • Referring Domains: Shows the number of domains unique ones that link to your site
  • Active Backlinks: Indicates the total number of backlinks assets that point to your website
Backlinks Report

To go deeper into the analysis, the tool allows you generate a detailed report of each backlink, providing you with the following metrics for each link:

  • Website: Generate a report of the website URL that links to your page.
  • Follow / No-follow: Indicates if the link is "Follow", which is useful for search engines, or "No-follow", which indicates that the website does not want the crawler follow the link.
  • Domain Authority (DA): Metric developed by Moz which measures the probability that a domain completely positioned in search engines.
  • Page Authority (PA): It is a metric that predicts what how well a specific page will rank in the search results. It is measured on a scale from 1 to 100, where higher values ​​indicate greater ranking ability.
  • Website Popularity Rank (WPR): Range of popularity of the website provided by Tranco. A Lower number indicates better ranking.
  • First Crawl: The first time the search engine crawled this URL. Last Crawl: The last time the search engine updated its tracking for this URL.
